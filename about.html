About Women in Computing Archive

The Women in Computing Archive is a digital initiative dedicated to commemorating and celebrating the groundbreaking contributions of women in the field of computer science. Inspired by my 'CSE 351' course, where I first learned about the ENIAC programmers, this project aims to preserve history, educate others, and empower future generations of female coders.

As someone who did not grow up in a tech-oriented environment, my perspective on computing and its pioneers has transformed over time. I initially viewed programming as intimidating, but through collaboration, problem-solving, and perseverance, I discovered its beauty. This project is my way of ensuring that women's contributions to computing are remembered and accessible to everyone.

This archive is more than just a collection of stories—it is a platform for learning, inspiration, and engagement. From Ada Lovelace and Grace Hopper to modern-day trailblazers, our mission is to highlight the achievements that have shaped the digital world. We invite you to explore, contribute, and be a part of this growing archive.
